One such visa is the\u00a0fianc\u00e9 visa<\/a>, or K-1 nonimmigrant visa, which may be available for a foreign citizen who wishes to marry a U.S. citizen. If you are thinking about applying for a fianc\u00e9 visa, it is important to understand all the eligibility requirements that will apply to both the immigrant fianc\u00e9 and the U.S. citizen sponsor.<\/p>\n

Eligible Relationship Status<\/h3>\n

The first requirement for eligibility for a fianc\u00e9 visa is that the couple must be legally able to get married after the foreign citizen enters the United States. This means that both partners must meet all legal requirements for marriage in the state where the marriage will take place. That is, they must meet the applicable age requirements, and they must not be currently married. If they had been married in the past, they will need to show that the marriage has been legally terminated, which may be done by providing a copy of a divorce decree or the death certificate for a deceased spouse.<\/p>\n

To demonstrate that they are getting married for valid reasons, the couple will usually need to show that they have met in person at some point within two years before applying for a K-1 visa. This meeting may have taken place in the United States or another country. In some cases, exceptions to this requirement may be available, although they must usually be based on long-standing customs. For example, if a couple is entering into an arranged marriage, and the practices that are often followed in the foreign fianc\u00e9’s country do not involve a face-to-face meeting beforehand, a fianc\u00e9 visa may be granted without the need to fulfill this requirement. Exemptions may also be available if the requirement to travel outside the United States to meet a fianc\u00e9 would involve extreme hardship for a U.S. citizen.<\/p>\n

U.S Citizen Sponsor Requirements<\/h3>\n

The U.S. citizen partner who is sponsoring their foreign fianc\u00e9 must be at least 18 years old, and they must have sufficient financial resources to support their foreign partner while the couple is living together in the United States. In addition, they must provide proof of their U.S. citizenship, which may include a valid passport, birth certificate, or\u00a0naturalization<\/a>\u00a0certificate.<\/p>\n

Foreign Partner Requirements<\/h3>\n

The foreign fianc\u00e9 will need to provide proof of their identity, such as official government identification documents. They will also need to attend an interview with a consular officer at the U.S. embassy or consulate in the country where they are currently living. The officer will review their documents to ensure that they are eligible for immigration to the U.S. and that there are no reasons for\u00a0inadmissibility<\/a>. After a visa is approved, the foreign fianc\u00e9 will come to a port of entry, where they will be inspected by customs officials. After they are approved for entry, they must get married to the U.S. citizen sponsor within 90 days.<\/p>\n
