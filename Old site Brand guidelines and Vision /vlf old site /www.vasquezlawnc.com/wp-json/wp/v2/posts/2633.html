The U.S. was founded on the basis of freedom, and here oppressed individuals can find the safety that would otherwise elude them.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

People come to the U.S. to seek <\/span>asylum<\/span><\/a> as we are a country that values security and liberty\u2013however, seeking asylum is also a complicated legal process that requires a lot of time, paperwork, and legal understanding. The lawyers of Vasquez Law Firm, PLLC, endeavor to help people accomplish their goals for immigrating to the U.S. so that they can establish better lives for themselves and their families.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Asylum seekers are among some of the most vulnerable people immigrating to the U.S., which is why Vasquez Law Firm has created this blog to outline the broad strokes of the process of seeking asylum so that you can better understand what\u2019s in store for yourself or your loved ones.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Read on to find out more about asylum seekers in America and the circumstances in which one would qualify.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

What Is Asylum?<\/b><\/p>\n

Asylum is a form of legal protection for foreign nationals who come to the U.S. seeking refuge from persecution related to their political position, religion, race, nationality, or membership in a particular social group. In order to seek protection in the U.S., the person seeking asylum (the asylee) must have a reasonable belief that their safety is in danger should they stay in their home country.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Seeking asylum status<\/span><\/a> gives the asylee recourse for legally establishing themselves in the U.S, as well as for other benefits that include:<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Safety from deportation and persecution<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Work authorization\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Opportunity to apply for a green card<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Opportunity to bring family to the U.S.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Opportunity to apply for a social security card and other documentation<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Opportunities to apply for healthcare<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Ability for overseas travel<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Sometimes the word \u201crefugee\u201d is used interchangeably for people who are seeking asylum, but the key difference is that refugees have not yet entered the U.S. whereas asylees are already here and applying for the protections that asylum status affords. Otherwise, asylum seekers meet the same standard of definition as refugees.<\/span><\/p>\n

    Who Can And Cannot File For Asylum?<\/b><\/p>\n

    In order to be granted asylum status, applicants must meet certain eligibility requirements. You could be eligible to seek asylum if you:<\/span><\/p>\n

      \n
    • Have come to the U.S. for protection from persecution<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Are inside the United States or at a port of entry<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Have been in the U.S. for less than a year, except in specific circumstances<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Have not been previously denied for asylum<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Have not passed through a safe third country, such as Canada<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Do not pose a danger to citizens of the U.S.<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Have not committed a serious crime (not all crimes will bar you from asylum)<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      If you meet this criteria, you have the opportunity to apply for asylum. If you have been in the U.S. for more than a year, you cannot apply for asylum, except in certain cases such as if you did not initially fear to return to your home country but do now because of new social or political activity, had an acute medical emergency, was a victim of human trafficking, and other circumstances.<\/span><\/p>\n

      The Process For Asylum<\/b><\/p>\n

      There are two methods with which asylum seekers can apply:<\/span><\/p>\n

        \n
      • Affirmative asylum<\/b>: Affirmative asylum is for people who are seeking asylum who are not currently going through removal proceedings. (You also cannot apply for affirmative asylum if you are an unaccompanied child in removal proceedings or have already submitted an application that is currently pending.) If the application is denied and you do not have legal status in the U.S., removal proceedings will begin against you.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Defensive asylum<\/b>: This type of filing is for people who are currently going through removal proceedings in the U.S. If you have previously been denied affirmative asylum, you can reapply for defensive asylum.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        Within a year of your arrival to the U.S., you can begin the process of applying for asylum by filing <\/span>Form I-589<\/span><\/a>. There is no fee to apply. You will need to prove that you meet the standards for asylum, which includes an asylum interview for which you can secure legal counsel to accompany you. After this interview, the decision might take as long as two weeks, or you will get your results by mail.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

        All in all, your journey to secure asylum in the U.S. can take years to complete. After you have been granted asylum, you will be immediately authorized to work in the U.S. In addition, you can apply for a green card after one year using <\/span>Form I-485<\/span><\/a>.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

        Families Of Asylum Seekers<\/b><\/p>\n

        If you have secured asylum in the U.S., you can go through the process to bring your family to the U.S using <\/span>Form I-730<\/span><\/a>. This process does not include a fee. If you are attempting to bring your children over to the U.S., they must be under 21 and unmarried to qualify. Your deadline for petitioning on behalf of your family is two years after you have been granted asylum except in exceptional circumstances.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n
