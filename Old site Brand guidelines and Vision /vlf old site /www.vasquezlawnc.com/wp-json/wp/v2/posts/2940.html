What to Do If You Get into An Accident with an Emergency Vehicle<\/h2>\n

Getting into an accident with an emergency vehicle can be a stressful and confusing experience. Some tips to recovering from such an accident include:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Stay calm and safe \u2013 First and foremost, ensure everyone\u2019s safety. If possible, move to a safe location and check for injuries. Call 911 immediately to report the accident and request medical assistance.<\/li>\n
  • Exchange information \u2013 Just like any other accident, exchange information with the driver of the emergency vehicle. This includes names, contact details, insurance information, and vehicle registration. Cooperate, but avoid discussing or admitting fault at the scene.<\/li>\n
  • Document the scene \u2013 Take pictures and videos of the accident scene, vehicle damages, and any relevant traffic signals or road conditions. Gather contact information from witnesses who can provide an unbiased account of the accident.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Are Emergency Vehicles Exempt from Traffic Laws?<\/h2>\n

    Emergency vehicles are given certain privileges under North Carolina law to perform their duties effectively. However, these exemptions come with specific conditions. Examples of traffic law exemptions for emergency vehicles include:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Exceed speed limits when responding to emergencies.<\/li>\n
    • Proceed through red lights or stop signs after ensuring it is safe to do so.<\/li>\n
    • Travel against the flow of traffic or on the shoulder of a road when necessary.<\/li>\n
    • Park in restricted areas to carry out emergency functions.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Emergency Vehicles Still Must Exercise a Duty of Care<\/h2>\n

      While they do have some leeway, emergency vehicle operators are required to drive with regard to the safety of all road users. Negligent driving, even if responding to an emergency, can result in liability for damages and injuries.<\/p>\n

      Responsibilities of a Private Motorist<\/h2>\n

      As a private motorist, you must:<\/p>\n

        \n
      • Yield to the right-of-way of emergency vehicles displaying flashing lights and sounding sirens.<\/li>\n
      • Move<\/a>\u00a0to the side of the road and come to a complete stop, allowing the emergency vehicle to pass.<\/li>\n
      • Avoid obstructing emergency vehicles and provide them with a clear path.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
