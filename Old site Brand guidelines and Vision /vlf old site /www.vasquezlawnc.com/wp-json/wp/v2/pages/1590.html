Non-immigrant and Immigrant Visas<\/h3>\n

Foreign nationals who wish to come to the United States for an extended period of time must obtain a visa. Temporary, or non-immigrant visas, are available to those coming to the U.S. for reasons such as tourism, education, or work-related purposes. For those who want to remain in the United States permanently, an immigrant visa is required. <\/span>Immigration reform<\/b> are available for employment-based reasons, but the majority of immigrant visas are granted to <\/span>immediate relatives<\/span><\/a> and other <\/span>family members<\/span><\/a> of U.S. citizens and current <\/span>lawful permanent residents<\/span><\/a>.<\/span><\/p>\n

The Immigrant Visa Process<\/h3>\n

Most immigrant visa petitions are filed in the United States by U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents (green card holders) who reside here. If you are looking to sponsor a family member\u2019s petition for an <\/span>first generation immigrant<\/b> visa<\/span>, you will need to follow these steps:<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Be sure that your family member qualifies for a visa.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Gather the information and documents required by Form I-130, Petition for Alien Relative.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Complete the I-130 package and submit it to the United States Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Upon receipt of the official notice that USCIS has approved the petition, the State Department\u2019s National Visa Center (NVC) will open a case. If a visa of the requested type is available, an immigrant visa number will be assigned.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Complete NVC processing and pay all of the required fees. As part of the NVC processing, you must file an affidavit of support showing that your family member will not need government assistance upon arrival, or you can also get an <\/span>immigration lawyer free consultation<\/b> for the processing of your immigrant visa.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Have your family member complete an immigrant visa interview at the U.S. embassy or consulate indicated by the NVC.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Following the interview, the application for an immigrant visa may be approved. The immigrant visa fee must then be paid.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • The visa holder may then travel to the United States and become a lawful permanent resident.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    The process of obtaining a visa is likely to take between 8 and 16 months, assuming that there is a visa of the appropriate type available. <\/span>The importance of understanding the differences between <\/span>emigrate vs immigrating<\/b> and knowing the proper process cannot be understated.<\/span><\/p>\n
