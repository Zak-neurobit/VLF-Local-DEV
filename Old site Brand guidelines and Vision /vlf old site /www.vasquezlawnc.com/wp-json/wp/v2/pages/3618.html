Types Of Workplace Injuries<\/strong><\/p>\n

Workplace injuries are common. They can be big or small, but <\/span>each one<\/span><\/a> deserves the proportional compensation from your employer. Though you might think that workers\u2019 comp applies only to factories with heavy machinery or construction sites where people often slip and fall, the truth is that workplace injuries happen all over America, and many of these employees need legal recourse to access what they are owed.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Here are some examples of common injuries that can happen in your office or site:<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Overexertion and heavy lifting<\/strong>: This is the <\/span>most common type of injury<\/span><\/a> on the job. If you are trying to lift heavy objects that weigh too much or with the wrong technique, you can become seriously injured.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Repetitive motion injuries<\/strong>: Doing the same thing over and over again, even if that movement happens to be very small, can cause muscle deterioration and lead to strain that could turn into debilitating permanent injury.   • Equipment-related injuries<\/strong>: Working with equipment can go dangerously wrong. From negligence to malfunction to improper maintenance and chemical corrosion, there are a whole host of dangers that come with working with machinery.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Construction site injuries<\/strong><\/a>: In addition to equipment related injuries, construction sites can be dangerous areas thanks to falls, explosions, and structural collapses. There is a reason people often think of construction when they think of workers\u2019 comp!<\/span><\/li>\n
