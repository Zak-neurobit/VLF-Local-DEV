Roselyn V. Torrellas<\/h1>\n

[\/et_pb_text][et_pb_text _builder_version=”4.25.1″ _module_preset=”default” text_font_size=”20px” text_line_height=”1.5em” hover_enabled=”0″ global_colors_info=”{}” text_font=”Montserrat||||||||” custom_css_free_form=”selector ul li{padding: 0px;}” sticky_enabled=”0″]<\/p>\n

Roselyn Torrellas was born in Barquisimeto, Venezuela, and is fluent in Spanish. She and her family immigrated to the United States when she was a teenager. Roselyn lived one year in Miami, Florida before her family relocated to Greensboro, North Carolina. When Roselyn graduated from high school, her family was still in the process of obtaining legal status in the United States. Therefore, she was unable to attend university immediately after graduation like some of her peers.<\/p>\n

Roselyn knew she wanted to become a lawyer at a very young age. After high school graduation, she decided she wanted to pursue immigration law. Her personal experience allows her to empathize with her clients while guiding them through the complex and intricate aspects of immigration law.<\/p>\n

Roselyn attended the University of North Carolina at Greensboro, where she earned her undergraduate degree in Political Science with a minor in Sociology. After graduation, she worked full-time for an immigration attorney in Greensboro. It was during this time that she decided to pursue her law degree. She attended the evening program at North Carolina Central University School of Law in Durham, where she earned her Juris Doctor in 2009.<\/p>\n

In her free time, Roselyn enjoys being outdoors as much as possible. She loves hiking, swimming, or just having a relaxing day at the park with her daughter. She also enjoys reading a good book and traveling outside of the United States at least once a year.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Roselyn has helped thousands of people while working in Miami, Florida, Denver, Colorado, Winston-Salem, Raleigh, and Smithfield, North Carolina. She offers her community legal counsel in various areas of immigration law, including: citizenship, adjustment of status, family petitions, DACA, asylum, and more. You can be assured that Roselyn is and will be honest, professional, and organized when working with you.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n
