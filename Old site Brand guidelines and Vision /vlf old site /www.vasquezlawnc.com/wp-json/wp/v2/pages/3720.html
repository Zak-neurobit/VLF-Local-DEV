A Short Guide To Workplace Injuries<\/strong><\/p>\n

For many people, they imagine workplace injuries are limited to construction sites and factories, which is not always the case. In fact, workplace injuries can happen in restaurants, offices, warehouses, and more. Injuries can be big or small, but all deserve the full amount of compensation needed in order to heal.<\/span><\/p>\n

Here are a few common types of workplace injuries:<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Overexertion and heavy lifting<\/strong>: These types of injuries are among the <\/span>most common types of injuries<\/span><\/a> in U.S. workplaces. Lifting heavy objects, especially incorrectly, carries risks that include sprains, breaks, and debilitating back injuries.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Repetitive motion injuries<\/strong>: Do you type all day? Do you find yourself performing the same basic tasks? Though it might not seem like it, doing the same movements over and over again can cause muscle deterioration, sprain, and eventually permanent injury. You might not think that typing carries a huge risk, for example, but it can lead to carpal tunnel syndrome! Other similar small repetitive tasks can also make a difference for your health.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Equipment-related injuries<\/strong>: Sometimes, equipment backfires, and it can mean serious repercussions for the people who work with it. Whether the reason is negligence, malfunction, improper maintenance, or chemical corrosion, working with equipment of any kind can be a risk to your health.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Construction site injuries<\/strong><\/a>: There is a reason people think of construction sites when they think of workplace injury. In addition to the risks posed by equipment, construction workers also have to worry about falls, explosions, and structural collapses.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Mental health impairment<\/strong>: If you were injured on the job and it led to a decline in your mental health, you are owed damages for treatment. Injury can lead to depression, anxiety, PTSD, and a whole host of other poor mental health conditions. In order to get you back on your feet, treating your mental health is just as important as treating your physical health.\u00a0<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Frequently Asked Questions<\/strong><\/p>\n

    Is workers\u2019 comp taxed?<\/strong><\/p>\n

    No, you will not have to pay taxes on your payout. The money is fully intended to help you with your medical needs.<\/span><\/p>\n

    Is there a time limit on when I need to submit my workers\u2019 comp claim?\u00a0<\/strong><\/p>\n

    In Florida, you have only 30 days following your injury to notify your employer of your injury. After that, you have two years in which to submit your claim for workers\u2019 comp.<\/span><\/p>\n

    Will workers\u2019 comp cover funeral costs if an employee dies as a result of a workplace injury?<\/strong><\/p>\n

    Yes. If a worker dies as a result of a workplace accident, workers\u2019 comp can cover funeral expenses.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n
