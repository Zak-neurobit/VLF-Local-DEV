Immigration Counsel for Families in North Carolina<\/h3>\n

The area of family-based immigration encompasses a wide range of cases in which United States citizens and lawful permanent residents (\u201cgreen card\u201d holders) seek to bring their family members and future spouses to the U.S. legally. According to U.S. immigration law, lawful permanent residents and legal citizens can serve as sponsors to help their relatives obtain permanent or temporary visas.
\nThis process is critical for families affected by illegal immigration concerns or those looking to assist loved ones in avoiding legal complications.<\/p>\n

Generally, the application process for family-based immigration is relatively straightforward. The process begins when a citizen or permanent resident files a completed Petition for Alien Relative (Form I-130) with the primary immigration agency of the U.S. government: United States Citizenship and Immigration Services, also known as USCIS. Once the petition is accepted by USCIS, the petitioner will be notified of a date after which the family members in question can apply for visas or green cards.<\/p>\n

Johnson County Lawyers Who Understand the Preference System for Family-Based Immigration<\/h3>\n

The United States government allows for approving a limited number of family visas and permanent resident status applications in a given year. Therefore, a preference system has been created for processing family immigration requests, even if you are hiring an immigration lawyer. Legal spouses, minor children, and parents of current U.S. citizens over 21 are immediate relatives, which means they are not included in the family preference system.
\nApplications for other relatives, however, are considered in order based on the status of the petitioner and the relationship:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • First preference is given to petitions filed by U.S. citizens for their unmarried children over the age of 21.<\/li>\n
  • Second preference is given to petitions filed by permanent residents for their spouses and children. Minor children receive preference over adult children.<\/li>\n
  • Third, preference is given to petitions filed by U.S. citizens for their married children and the immediate relatives of their married children.<\/li>\n
  • Fourth, preference is given to petitions filed by U.S. citizens for their siblings and the immediate relatives of their siblings.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
